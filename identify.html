Identify Common Pests

Use our quick reference guide to recognize the pests most commonly found in Alabama homes and businesses.

Ants

Small insects with segmented bodies and elbowed antennae. Look for long trails leading to food, soil mounds around foundations or wood shavings from carpenter ants.

Bed Bugs

Tiny, flat, reddish‑brown insects that hide in seams of mattresses and furniture. Signs include itchy welts, dark spots or shed skins on bedding and nearby crevices.

Cockroaches

Flattened, fast‑moving insects that prefer dark, damp areas. You may notice egg cases, pepper‑like droppings, a musty odor or sightings when you switch on the lights.

Mosquitoes

Slender flying insects that bite to feed on blood. Standing water, clogged gutters and dense vegetation provide breeding sites for these disease carriers.

Rodents

Mice and rats leave gnaw marks, droppings and greasy rub marks along walls. Listen for scratching noises in walls or attics, and look for nests made of shredded materials.

Termites

Wood‑eating insects that live in colonies. Watch for mud tubes on foundations, hollow‑sounding wood or swarming winged termites emerging indoors.

Spiders

Eight‑legged arachnids that build webs or hunt on foot. While many are beneficial, venomous species like black widows have distinctive markings and prefer secluded areas.

Wasps & Hornets

Stinging insects that build papery nests under eaves, in trees or in the ground. They can be aggressive if disturbed, so it’s best to leave removal to professionals.
